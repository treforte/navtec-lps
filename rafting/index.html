Before You Book

Common Questions

For young kids or first-timers, the Moab Rafting Half-Day (Class II–III) or Sport Boat Scenic Cruise (Class I) are great starting points. Ready for real whitewater? Westwater and Cataract Canyon step up to Class III through V. Tell us your group's ages and comfort level on the form below and we'll point you to the right trip.

Life jackets are worn at all times and required for every guest. Basic swimming comfort is recommended for Class III+ trips; the calm-water and Class I–II trips are appropriate for non-swimmers with a properly fitted life jacket.

The RHIB (Rigid Hull Inflatable Boat) is motorized and more maneuverable — it's how we're able to run rapids upstream and downstream on our Moab Rafting RHIB and Cataract One-Day trips. Paddle and oar rafts are non-motorized and used on our multi-day expeditions for a quieter, more traditional river experience.

All camping gear, meals, and guiding are included on overnight and multi-day trips — you bring your personal items and a sense of adventure. We'll send a full packing list once you book.

River corridors typically see less smoke impact than backcountry overlooks, but conditions can shift. We track air quality daily and offer free date changes if your specific date looks affected.

Absolutely — many guests pair a Colorado River trip with a Canyonlands or Arches 4x4 tour for a complete Moab itinerary. Mention it on the form below and we'll help you build a combo schedule.
